**Product type:** SaaS — subscription-based, monthly or yearly billing

## 5. Target Customer (ICP)

**Primary:** Indian SMBs and growth-stage businesses that run on conversations — they get daily enquiries and can't afford to miss them.

**Use cases / verticals:**
- **Real Estate & Property** — site visit enquiries, project detail sends, site visit scheduling
- **Clinics & Local Services** — pricing/service questions, booking confirmations, no receptionist needed
- **Agencies & SaaS** — pre-qualify leads from ads, route only sales-ready prospects to humans

**What makes them a fit:** They get enquiries daily, have a sales team, run Meta/Google ads, and are currently losing leads to slow responses or no-shows.

**Psychographic:** Open to automation, frustrated with manual work, care deeply about missed revenue more than cost.

## 11. Brand Voice & Tone

- **Confident, not arrogant** — make bold claims but back them with specifics
- **Direct** — lead with the outcome, not the feature
- **Technical credibility** — name the tech (SerpAPI, Perplexity AI, HubSpot) to build trust
- **Urgency without hype** — "hot leads go cold" is real, not manufactured pressure
- **No fluff** — avoid "game-changing", "revolutionary", "cutting-edge" — show, don't tell

**Avoid:** "leverage", "synergy", "robust", "seamless" (overused); generic stock-photo vibes; vague benefit claims without numbers

## 12. Competitive Landscape

- **Traditional agencies** — charge $3,000–$8,000/mo for similar outcomes; Bitlance undercuts on price and removes human dependency

**Key wedge:** Bitlance is an _autonomous system_, not a tool you manage.
